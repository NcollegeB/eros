How to start selling Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook ads for clients.

This is a practical starter system: choose a clear offer, set up accounts correctly, test creative, and report the metrics that actually guide decisions.

Setup Creative Launch Metrics Cadence

1. Choose the offer and account setup

Pick one starting service so the market understands what you do. A good first offer is a paid social launch sprint, monthly campaign management, or a creative testing package.

  • Define the client niche, campaign goal, budget range, and expected timeline.
  • Use Meta Business Suite for Facebook and Instagram, and TikTok Business Center for TikTok.
  • Request partner access to client ad accounts instead of using shared passwords.
  • Install pixels, conversion events, UTM links, and Google Analytics before launch.

2. Build creative before buying media

Most early ad accounts need better creative before they need advanced media buying. Start each client with a testing bank.

HooksProblem, outcome, proof
FormatsTalking head, product demo, customer story
AnglesPrice/value, speed, trust
CTAsBook now, shop now, get offer, learn more

3. Launch small and read the signal

Start with a focused test. Choose the objective that matches the business goal, keep the structure clean, and compare creative against creative.

  • Instagram and Facebook: use Meta campaigns for leads, sales, traffic, or awareness depending on the goal.
  • TikTok: test native-feeling short video first, then scale the hook and format that holds attention.
  • Budget: use enough daily spend to learn, often $20 to $50 per campaign for small tests.
  • Landing page: match the ad promise, proof, and call to action.

5. Report with decisions, not screenshots

Clients need to know what changed, what won, what lost, and what you are doing next. Keep a weekly cadence.

  1. Check delivery, spend, tracking, and rejected ads.
  2. Review creative performance by hook, format, and audience.
  3. Pause weak ads and shift budget toward winners.
  4. Build new creative from what the data showed.
  5. Send a short summary with next actions and client asks.
