# Social Ads Playbook

This is the practical operating guide behind the Northbeam Creative site.

## 1. Pick a Clear Starting Offer

Start with one service package before trying to sell every ad service at once.

- Offer: paid social launch sprint, monthly campaign management, or creative testing package
- Audience: local businesses, ecommerce brands, coaches, home services, restaurants, or another clear niche
- Outcome: leads booked, sales, store visits, app installs, or qualified calls
- Proof: even if you are new, use sample strategy decks, spec ads, audits, or a personal case study

## 2. Set Up the Business Infrastructure

For each client, make sure the account ownership is clean.

- Meta Business Suite for Facebook and Instagram
- TikTok Business Center for TikTok ads
- Ad account access through partner permissions, not shared passwords
- Pixel or event tracking installed on the website
- Conversion events defined before spend begins
- UTM links added to every ad
- Google Analytics or another analytics tool connected
- A shared reporting doc or dashboard

## 3. Build Creative Before Buying Media

Most small accounts do not fail because of obscure bidding settings. They fail because the ad creative is weak or mismatched to the offer.

Create a testing bank:

- 3 hooks: problem, outcome, proof
- 3 formats: talking head, product demo, customer story
- 3 angles: price/value, speed, trust
- 2 calls to action: book now, get offer, shop now, learn more

## 4. Launch Small, Then Read the Data

Start with focused tests instead of too many campaigns.

- Budget: enough to get signal, usually at least $20 to $50 per day per campaign
- Campaign objective: choose the real business goal, not vanity engagement
- Audiences: begin broad, then test retargeting once enough traffic exists
- Creative: launch multiple variants at once
- Landing page: align headline, offer, proof, and CTA with the ad

## 5. Weekly Optimization Cadence

Use the same rhythm every week.

- Monday: check spend, delivery, errors, and tracking
- Tuesday: review creative performance by hook and format
- Wednesday: pause weak ads and shift budget toward winners
- Thursday: build new creative from what the data showed
- Friday: send the client a short report with next actions

## 6. Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Formula | What It Tells You |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CPM | spend / impressions x 1,000 | Cost to reach the market |
| CTR | clicks / impressions | Whether the creative gets attention |
| CPC | spend / clicks | Cost of qualified traffic |
| CVR | conversions / clicks | Whether the page and offer convert |
| CPA | spend / conversions | Cost to acquire the action |
| ROAS | revenue / spend | Revenue efficiency for ecommerce |
| Hook rate | 3-second views / impressions | Whether the opening grabs attention |
| Hold rate | thruplays or 50 percent views / video starts | Whether viewers keep watching |
| CAC | sales and marketing cost / new customers | Real acquisition cost |
| LTV:CAC | customer lifetime value / CAC | Whether growth is healthy |

## 7. How to Explain Results to Clients

Do not only report numbers. Report what changed and what you will do next.

- What we tested
- What won
- What lost
- What we learned about the audience
- What budget moved
- What creative we are making next
- What the client needs to provide

## 8. First 30 Days

- Week 1: onboarding, tracking, offer review, creative plan
- Week 2: build ads, landing page notes, launch campaigns
- Week 3: first optimization, kill obvious losers, produce new variants
- Week 4: report, scale winners, plan the next creative cycle

The goal is not to guess perfectly on day one. The goal is to build a repeatable system that turns audience response into better creative and better media buying.
